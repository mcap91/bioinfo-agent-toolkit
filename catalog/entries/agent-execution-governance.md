## What it says

Argues that most AI agents (email assistants, meeting summaries, travel planners) fail to become daily-use tools because they stop at producing answers instead of being accountable for executing actions. The missing infrastructure is around trust and execution, not intelligence.

Infrastructure requirements identified: authenticated agent identity, policy-governed execution, verifiable results, auditable action receipts, appropriate routing, and full observability of agent behavior.

The thesis: the "killer app" for agents will be quiet automation of repetitive operational work inside businesses, where every action is authenticated, governed, and auditable — not consumer-facing assistants.

## Key takeaways

- The intelligence gap is closing; the trust/execution/integration gap is what blocks real-world agent adoption
- Agents that automate operational work need the same auditability infrastructure as human workers
- Overlaps with the CASTEM framework (Credible, Auditable, Supervised, Traceable, Explainable, Monitored) and harness engineering concepts (guides + sensors)
